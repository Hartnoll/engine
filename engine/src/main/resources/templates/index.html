Virtual ICE lab

Welcome to the online recreation of the combustion engine lab for the engineering department at the University of Bristol. On this page you will find links to the relevant page(s) to complete the lab as well as some pre-lab information and instructions on how to use the application we have created.

Pre-lab information

The purpose of this lab is to study the effects that rpm has on the internal combustion engine.

This application has been created as a digital alternative to the 'Engines Laboratory' so that it can be carried out in an online setting.
Make sure to have completed all of the pre-lab exercises and read the information that is found on the unit page on blackboard.

Application Tutorial

Upon completing the pre-lab exercises and reading up on the required material it is now time to complete the lab.
Once opening the control page you will see a video on the left and a dial along with a stopwatch on the right. Click the dial to change the RPM of the engine in the video and then use the 'start/stop' button next to the stopwatch to start the video and watch the fuel being used. Then use the controls above the stopwatch to make the required readings.
Underneath the video and stopwatch you will see a green button to 'add a new reading' click this in order to add a new row to the table so that you can make a record of your readings.
Below that you will see a Calculations area, use this feature to input your answers to the required formulas one step at a time and check they are correct at the end using the 'next and 'check' buttons.
